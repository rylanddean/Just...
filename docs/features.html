Features

Everything it needs.
Nothing it doesn't.

The Reader. The Reflect Window. The Brain. The Streak. Feeds and newsletters. Every feature earns its place by making the habit easier to keep.

The Reader

The reader disappears. The words stay.

When you open a link, Just… fetches the article, strips images and ads, and renders the text in a clean reading view. No sidebars. No related content. No progress bars. Just the article.

Playfair Display at a comfortable size on a warm dark background — the same warmth as a reading lamp, not the cold blue of a screen.

On the rare occasion an article cannot be fetched, a built-in browser opens so the content is still accessible — never a dead end.

"Chrome is noise."

The reader is designed around a single principle: every visible element that is not the article text is a distraction. Just… removes them all.

Themes & Night Mode

Six themes. Night mode applied automatically.

The default theme is Ember — warm amber on near-black, the closest digital equivalent to a reading lamp. For those who prefer a cooler palette or a light background, five additional themes are available in Settings: Slate, Dusk, Sage, Sepia, and Paper. Sepia and Paper are light themes suited for reading in daylight.

Night mode is a separate layer. When it activates — automatically at 9PM, or at whatever time you set — Just… switches to the Night theme regardless of your chosen theme. It deactivates at 6AM. You can override it to always on or always off from Settings.

Text size and line spacing are adjustable from inside the reader itself. Tap the text size control in the top bar and drag to your preference. The setting persists across all reading sessions.

"The warmth of a reading lamp, not the cold blue of a screen."

Colour temperature affects how comfortable reading is at night. Just…'s dark themes are warm by default — amber and cream rather than blue and white.

The Night theme is not available in the theme picker — it is applied automatically by the Night Mode schedule. This is intentional: Night Mode is a habit, not a setting you toggle mid-read.

The Reflect Window

60 seconds. One thought. That is all it takes.

After every article, a short writing window opens. A prompt appears as placeholder text — it disappears the moment you start. The timer counts down but pauses while you write.

Skip is always visible. There is no minimum word count. One word is enough. The Brain remembers either way.

On devices with Apple Intelligence, the prompt is generated from the article itself — a question specific to what you just read, not a generic nudge. On all other devices, four rotating prompts keep the window fresh.

"Reading without reflecting is like exercising without sleeping. The growth happens in the pause."

The reflect window is grounded in the testing effect — one of the most replicated findings in cognitive science. Writing or speaking one thought after reading is enough to significantly improve long-term retention, compared to reading more content passively.

Voice reflection is equally effective as writing, per the production effect established by MacLeod and Forrin (2017). Just… supports both.

The Brain

Every entry stays. The Brain never shrinks.

Every article you read and every reflection you write is stored in the Brain — your personal knowledge base. Not a folder. Not a tag system. Just a growing record of what you have read and what it made you think.

As the Brain grows, it earns a new rank: Curious, Reader, Thinker, Scholar, Polymath, Luminary. Rank is a title, not a level. There is no confetti. Just a quiet line: "Your Brain is now a Thinker."

Search your Brain at any time — by article title, domain, or the exact words you wrote in a reflection. The Brain also surfaces a weekly Revisit card: one older entry, chosen at random from entries with reflections, surfaced so the memory reactivates.

"Over months and years, a Brain with hundreds of entries is not just a reading log. It is a map of how your thinking has grown."

The Brain is the long game. The streak is binary — you showed up or you didn't. The Brain compounds. Each entry is a small anchor. Return to an old reflection and the memory reactivates. Ebbinghaus' forgetting curve works against you. The Brain works with you.

The Streak

Did you show up today?

Reading at least one article per day keeps the streak alive. No minimum reading time. No minimum reflection length. Binary — you showed up or you didn't.

The streak resets at 3AM, not midnight — the same grace window shared across all Just family apps. Late nights happen.

When the streak ends, the copy is plain: "Your streak ended. Start again." No mourning. No punishment. The Brain keeps every entry regardless.

"The streak is a question, not a reward."

Just… does not celebrate streaks with animations or points. The amber number at the top of the screen is a quiet accountability tool — a single question asked every day: did you show up?

Notifications are opt-in and requested only after the streak reaches 3 days. Never on first launch.

Feeds & Newsletters

Your reading, delivered. No inbox required.

Just… reads RSS and Atom feeds from any source — news, blogs, research, podcasts — and surfaces new items directly in your reading queue. No algorithm. No engagement bait. Just the sources you chose.

Newsletter support goes further. Just… generates a private reading address for each newsletter you follow. Subscribe to any newsletter using that address instead of your personal email. New editions appear in your Feeds tab automatically, ready to read and reflect on like any other article.

Not sure what to follow? Just… includes a built-in directory of 280+ curated newsletters across Technology, Programming, AI, Design, Business, Security, and more. Tap Browse → Newsletters, pick one, and subscribe without ever leaving the app.

"No inbox. No algorithm. Just the sources you chose."

The newsletter flow is entirely in-app. Once you select a newsletter, Just… generates your reading address, opens the newsletter's sign-up page, and copies the address to your clipboard — ready to paste. No switching to Safari. No manual copying.

RSS feeds are detected automatically when you paste a website URL. Categories are assigned using Apple Intelligence on supported devices, or a built-in classifier on all others.

Digest

A week of reading, at a glance.

The Digest tab shows everything your feeds have published in the last seven days — today, yesterday, and earlier — without you having to scroll through each feed individually. It is a browse surface, not a queue.

When article grading is enabled, the Digest can filter out noise automatically. Just… uses Apple Intelligence on supported devices to score each article as Signal or Noise before it reaches the Digest — hiding aggregated takes, press releases, and listicles so only the articles worth reading surface first. Grading runs in the background after each fetch.

Tap any article in the Digest to add it to your reading queue.

"Browse in the Digest. Read in the queue."

The Digest and the queue serve different purposes. The Digest is where you decide what to read next. The queue is where you actually read it — one article at a time, with the reflect window waiting at the end.

Noise articles are hidden, not deleted. Disable "Hide noise" in Settings at any time to see everything your feeds published.

Picked for you

The algorithm is your Brain, not ours.

As your Brain grows, Just… learns from what you read and what you thought about it. Once you have five or more Brain entries, it begins surfacing articles from your feeds that match your reading history.

Picks appear in a dedicated section at the top of your reading queue, labelled "Picked for you." They are ranked by relevance to your Brain — not by engagement, recency, or popularity.

On devices with Apple Intelligence, picks are generated from the semantic content of your reflections. On all other devices, they match on topic keywords extracted from your Brain entries.

"The more you read and reflect, the better your picks become."

Your reflections describe what actually interested you — not just what you clicked. Picks are drawn from that signal. This is the compounding effect of the Brain made visible.

The queue is honest: "Read more to improve your picks." appears until you have enough Brain entries to generate meaningful recommendations.

Pricing

Free. No strings.

Just… is free. No subscription. No in-app purchases. No locked features. Everything described on this page is included — the Reader, the Reflect Window, the Brain, the Streak, feeds, newsletters, themes, Digest, and picks.

Your Brain is stored on your device and in your personal iCloud account. Just… never has access to your reflections.
