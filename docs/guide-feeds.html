Guide 03

Feeds and
newsletters.

How to bring your reading to Just… — RSS feeds, newsletters, the Digest, and more.

Overview

Content that comes to you.

Feeds let you follow any RSS source — news sites, blogs, research — and surface new articles directly in Just…. No algorithm. No engagement bait. Just the sources you chose.

Newsletter support works differently: Just… generates a private reading address for each newsletter. Subscribe using that address instead of your personal email. New editions appear in Just… automatically.

Everything from your feeds appears in the Digest tab, where you can browse, queue articles for later, or read them immediately.

RSS feeds

Adding an RSS feed.

01

Open the Feeds tab.

Tap the Feeds icon at the bottom of the screen.

02

Tap the + button.

An add feed sheet opens.

03

Paste the feed URL and tap Add.

Paste an RSS feed URL directly, or paste a website URL — Just… detects the RSS feed automatically. If a site has multiple feeds, choose which one to follow.

04

The feed is added.

Recent articles appear in the Digest within a few moments. On supported devices, Just… assigns a category automatically. Change it by tapping the feed and selecting a different category.

Browsing the directory

Not sure what to follow? Tap Browse → Feeds in the Feeds tab to see a curated list of sources across Technology, Business, Science, Design, and more. Tap any source to add it instantly.

Newsletters

Following a newsletter.

Just… includes a directory of 280+ curated newsletters. For newsletters not in the directory, add them manually with any newsletter website URL.

01

Tap Browse → Newsletters in the Feeds tab.

Search by name or browse by category — Technology, Programming, AI, Design, Business, Security, and more.

02

Tap the newsletter, then tap Subscribe.

To add one not in the directory, tap + → Newsletter and paste the newsletter's website URL instead.

03

Give the feed a name.

The newsletter's sign-up form loads within Just….

04

Your reading address appears at the top — already copied.

This is a unique email address Just… generated for this newsletter. Paste it into the subscription form on the page below.

05

Complete the newsletter's sign-up form, then tap Done.

The feed is added. New editions appear in the Digest automatically once the newsletter starts sending to your reading address.

Reading address

Your reading address is a unique email scoped to one newsletter. Your personal email is never involved. To find it again, long-press the newsletter in the Feeds tab and tap Copy reading address.

The Digest

Browse your feeds at a glance.

The Digest tab shows everything your feeds have published within your article retention window — grouped into Today, Yesterday, and Earlier. It is a browse surface, not a reading queue.

Read immediately

Tap an article to open it in the reader right away — no queue required. After reflecting, the article is marked as seen and removed from the Digest.

Add to queue

Tap the + next to any article to add it to your reading queue for later. The article stays in the Digest until you read and reflect on it.

Filter by topic

Just… extracts topic tags from your feeds automatically. Tap a topic pill at the top of the Digest to show only articles tagged with that topic.

Hide noise

On supported devices, Just… grades articles automatically. Enable Hide noise to filter out press releases, listicles, and promotional content.

Article retention

By default, Just… keeps two days of feed articles in the Digest. Change this in Settings → Feeds → Article retention — options are 1, 2, 3, 5, or 7 days. Newsletter editions are always retained for 30 days, regardless of this setting.

Daily Edition

A curated reading session from your feeds.

Each day, Just… selects three to five articles from your feeds and assembles them into a Daily Edition — a short, focused reading session ready at the top of your Queue tab. Articles are chosen for quality and filtered for noise before the edition is built. If your Brain has five or more entries, relevance to your reading history shapes the selection further.

01

Find "Today's Edition" at the top of your Queue.

The card shows how many articles are in today's selection and tracks your progress as you work through them.

02

Review the articles.

Each article is listed with its source and a short summary. You can see the full edition before committing to read it.

03

Tap Begin Reading.

Just… opens the first article in the full reader. Read it, reflect, and the edition advances automatically to the next.

04

"Edition complete."

Every article read through the edition is saved to your Brain. Tomorrow, a fresh edition replaces it.

Resuming

The edition remembers your place. Close it mid-session and the card shows your progress — "2 of 4 read." Tap to continue where you left off.

Favourite feeds

Auto-queue from feeds you trust.

Favourited feeds are pinned at the top of the Feeds tab and new articles from them are added to your reading queue automatically as they arrive. Use this for sources you read consistently.

01

Long-press any feed in the Feeds tab.

A context menu appears.

02

Tap Auto-queue new articles.

The feed moves to the top of your Feeds list. New articles are added to your reading queue automatically.

03

To stop, long-press again and tap Stop auto-queuing.

The feed stays in your list — just without auto-queuing.

Managing feeds

Renaming, archiving, and removing feeds.

Rename

Long-press the feed and tap Rename.

Archive

Long-press and tap Archive. The feed is hidden but not deleted. Find archived feeds in a collapsed section at the bottom of the Feeds tab.

Remove

Swipe left on the feed and tap Delete, or long-press and tap Delete. The feed and all its articles are removed. This cannot be undone.

Change category

Tap the feed to open its detail view, then tap the category label to change it.
