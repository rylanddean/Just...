Your
Brain.

Everything your Brain stores, how to search it, and what the ranks mean.

What the Brain is

A record that never shrinks.

Every article you read in Just… is logged in the Brain — along with your reflection, if you wrote one. There is no organising required. Over time it becomes a personal record of what you have read and what it made you think.

Entries One per article. Includes article title, source domain, date, and your reflection (if written).
Quotes Passages saved by long-pressing text in the reader. Stored separately and never expire.
Rank A title earned as the Brain grows. Updates automatically as entries accumulate.
Topics Concept keywords extracted from your entries. Used for filtering and the Insights panel.
Heatmap A 52-week activity grid. Filled squares are days with at least one read.
Browsing

Your reading history.

The Brain tab shows entries in reverse chronological order — newest first. Each card shows the article title, source, relative date, and a preview of your reflection.

Tap any entry to open a detail view with the full reflection and a link back to the original article. Swipe left on any entry to delete it. Deletion cannot be undone.

Search

Find any entry.

A search bar appears at the top of the Brain tab. It searches across article titles, source domains, and the full text of your reflections — including entries from years ago.

On devices with Apple Intelligence, search understands meaning. Search for a concept or a phrase and Just… surfaces entries related to that idea even if the exact words don't match.

The search field clears when you navigate away. There is no persistent search history.

Topic filters

Filter by topic.

Just… extracts concept keywords from your Brain entries and displays them as tappable pills in the Insights panel. Tap any topic to show only entries tagged with that concept. Tap All to clear the filter.

Topics are generated automatically — no manual tagging required. On devices with Apple Intelligence, topics reflect the semantic content of the article and reflection. On other devices, they are based on extracted keywords.

Reading patterns

The Insights panel shows how often each topic appears in your Brain. The pattern of what you keep returning to becomes visible over time.

Quotes

Passages worth keeping.

The Quotes section appears above the entry timeline in the Brain tab. It shows every passage you saved while reading — the full text in italic, the source domain, and the date.

Tap any quote card to open a detail view with the full passage and a link back to the original article. Swipe left to delete a quote.

To save a quote while reading, long-press any text in the reader and tap Keep this. See the reader guide for a step-by-step walkthrough.

Ranks

The Brain earns a title as it grows.

Rank is a title, not a level. When your Brain reaches a new rank, a quiet notification appears: "Your Brain is now a Thinker." No confetti. Just the fact.

Curious Where every Brain begins. 0 – 25 entries
Reader The habit is forming. 26 – 100
Thinker Reflection is a reflex now. 101 – 300
Scholar A year of consistent reading. 301 – 750
Polymath The breadth shows. 751 – 2000
Luminary A Brain worth revisiting. 2001+
Revisit

One older entry, surfaced each day.

The Revisit card appears in the Brain tab once your Brain contains enough entries. It surfaces one past entry — selected from entries that haven't been revisited recently — and gives you space to add a new thought alongside your original reflection.

Returning to an old entry reactivates its memory — more effective than reading new content passively. The card refreshes once per day.

Storage

Your Brain lives on your device.

All Brain entries are stored locally. No reflections are sent to any external server. If iCloud Drive is enabled, your Brain is backed up automatically and restores on a new device when you re-download Just….

Brain export to Markdown is on the roadmap. In the meantime, iCloud ensures your entries are safe if you switch devices.
