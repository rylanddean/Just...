Guide 02

Using the
reader.

Controls, themes, text size, saving quotes, and listening to articles.

Opening a link

Tap to read.

Tap any link card in the queue to open the reader. Just… fetches the article, removes images and ads, and renders the text in a clean reading view. A progress bar at the top fills as you scroll.

Once an article has been fetched, it is stored on your device. Re-opening it does not require a network connection.

Paywall or broken page

Some sites use paywalls or JavaScript rendering that prevents clean fetching. When this happens, tap the globe icon in the top bar to open the full site in a browser view — the content is always accessible.

Controls

The top bar.

A small set of controls lives in the top bar while you read.

× (close)

Exits the reader and returns to the queue without marking the article as read.

Domain · time

Shows the article's source domain and estimated reading time. Not interactive.

Waveform

Starts listen mode — the article is read aloud. See Listen mode below.

Aa

Opens text size controls. Drag to adjust font size and line spacing independently.

Globe

Opens the full article URL in a browser view within Just….

Copy

Copies the article URL to the clipboard.

Text size

Adjust the text to your preference.

Font size and line spacing are adjustable from inside the reader. Changes apply immediately and persist across all articles.

01

Tap the Aa button in the top bar.

A control panel slides in from the bottom of the screen.

02

Drag to adjust font size.

The first slider controls font size (range: 16–24pt). The default is 20pt.

03

Drag to adjust line spacing.

The second slider controls space between lines (range: 1.5–2.0×). Larger values give more breathing room between lines.

04

Tap anywhere to dismiss.

Your settings are saved immediately.

Themes

Six reading themes.

The default theme is Ember — warm amber on near-black, the closest digital equivalent to a reading lamp. Sepia and Paper are light themes suited for reading in bright conditions. Change your theme in Settings → Reader → Theme.

Ember Warm amber on near-black. Default. The warmth of a reading lamp.
Slate Cool grey on dark. Neutral and quiet.
Dusk Warm off-white on deep charcoal. Softer contrast than Ember.
Sage Muted green on dark. Easy on the eyes for long sessions.
Sepia Dark text on warm parchment. A light theme for daylight reading.
Paper Black on white. High contrast light theme.
Night mode

Automatic and adjustable.

Night mode applies a warm, very dark theme automatically at 9PM and deactivates at 6AM — regardless of which theme you have selected. It is a separate layer on top of your chosen theme, not a theme itself.

Adjust the start time, or override to always on or always off, in Settings → Reader → Night Mode.

Why it works this way

Night mode is a habit, not a setting you toggle mid-read. Keeping it automatic means the reader adjusts to the time of day on its own — you never have to think about it.

Saving quotes

Keep a passage that stayed with you.

Save any text from an article directly to the Quotes section of your Brain. Quotes are stored alongside your reading history and never expire.

01

Long-press any text in the article.

The system text selection handles appear.

02

Adjust the selection to cover the passage you want.

Drag the handles to expand or narrow the selection.

03

Tap Keep this from the menu.

A sheet appears with two options: Keep this to save to your Brain, or Send via Messages. Tap Keep this.

04

"Kept. Your Brain grows."

The quote is stored in the Quotes section of your Brain tab, tagged with the article title and date.

Listen mode

Listen instead of read.

Just… can read any article aloud using on-device text-to-speech. The current sentence is highlighted as it plays. Lock screen controls let you manage playback without opening the app.

01

Tap the waveform icon in the top bar.

The reader transitions to listen mode. The top bar is replaced by playback controls.

02

Playback starts from the beginning.

The current sentence is highlighted in amber and scrolls into view automatically. Use the skip-back 10s button to replay a passage.

03

Adjust playback speed.

Tap the speed control in the playback bar to cycle through speeds from 0.9× to 1.75×. Your preference is saved for future sessions.

04

Tap Done to finish.

Playback stops and the reflect window opens, the same as after reading manually.

Lock screen

The article title appears in Now Playing. Lock screen controls work the same as with music or podcasts. Incoming calls pause playback and resume automatically when the call ends.

After reading

The reflect window opens automatically.

When you reach the bottom of an article, pull up slightly to trigger the reflect window. A 60-second writing prompt opens. Write one thought, skip, or just save — the article is added to your Brain either way.

For a full walkthrough of the reflect window, see the Getting started guide.
