Guide 05

Streak and
notifications.

How the streak works, when it resets, and how to configure reminders.

How it works

One read per day. That is the whole thing.

Reading at least one article per day keeps the streak alive. No minimum reading time. No minimum reflection length. Binary — you showed up or you didn't.

The streak count appears in the Home tab, along with your longest streak ever and a row of dots for the past seven days. Filled dots are days with at least one read.

What counts Opening and finishing an article — not just adding one to the queue.
Reset time 3AM local time, not midnight. Reads between midnight and 3AM belong to the previous calendar day.
Minimum reads One per day by default. Change this in Settings → Reading Habit → Min reads per day (up to 7).
If streak breaks Your Brain keeps every entry regardless. The streak resets; the Brain does not.
The grace window

Late nights happen.

The streak resets at 3AM, not midnight. This is intentional — and shared across all Just family apps.

If you read at 11PM and again at 1AM, both reads belong to the same calendar day. The grace window means late-night reading sessions do not create accidental streak breaks.

At 3:01AM, the day turns. Reads from that point on belong to the new day.

At risk

"Your streak is at risk. Still time."

If you have an active streak but haven't read today, Just… sends a single notification at 9PM local time. It fires once per day and only when the streak is active. It does not fire if you have already read.

Notification permission

Just… requests notification permission after your streak reaches 3 days — never on first launch. If you declined earlier and want to enable it, go to iOS Settings → Notifications → Just….

Streak loss

Start again.

If the streak ends, a notification appears the following morning: "Your streak ended. Start again." Nothing more.

The Brain keeps every entry regardless of your streak status. Every read you have ever completed is still there. Pick up where you left off.

There is no streak freeze, no streak recovery, no grace day. Just the streak — and the decision to start again.

Notifications

What Just… sends and when.

Streak at risk

9PM local time if you haven't read today and have an active streak. "Your streak is at risk. Still time."

Streak lost

8AM the morning after a streak ends. "Your streak ended. Start again."

Brain rank up

Immediately after reflecting when the Brain earns a new rank. "Your Brain is now a Thinker."

Morning reminder

Optional. Configure in Settings → Notifications → Daily reminders. Off by default.

Evening reminder

Optional. A second reminder at your chosen time. Configure in Settings → Notifications → Daily reminders. Off by default.

Reminders

Setting up daily reminders.

Daily reminders are separate from streak notifications. They fire at your chosen time every day, regardless of whether you have already read.

01

Open Settings from the Home tab.

Tap the gear icon in the top-left corner of the Home tab.

02

Tap Notifications → Daily reminders.

Two toggles appear: morning and evening.

03

Enable the reminder and set the time.

Tap the toggle to enable. A time picker appears below. Defaults are 8AM and 8PM.

04

Grant notification permission if prompted.

If this is your first notification, iOS will ask for permission. Tap Allow. If you previously denied permission, go to iOS Settings → Notifications → Just… to enable it there first.

Turning off all notifications

Go to iOS Settings → Notifications → Just… and disable notifications entirely. Or disable individual toggles in Just… Settings → Notifications.
