Support

How can we help?

Common questions below. If yours isn't here, write to us directly.

Getting started

The basics.

How do I add a link to Just…?

The easiest way is to use the iOS Share Sheet. In Safari or any other app, tap the Share button and select Just… from the list. The link goes straight into your reading queue.

You can also add links manually from inside the app using the Add Link button in the queue view.

What happens after I finish reading?

A reflect window opens automatically. You have 60 seconds to write or speak one thought about what you just read. The timer pauses while you type or dictate. Skip is always visible — tap it any time to move on.

Whatever you write (or don't write) gets stored in your Brain.

Do I have to write a reflection?

No. Skip is always available. "Noted. The Brain remembers either way." — even a skipped article creates an entry in your Brain with the article title and date.

Can I read articles without an internet connection?

Articles are fetched when you tap to read them, so an active connection is required at that point. Once fetched, the content is available offline for the current session. Background pre-fetching is planned for a future version.

Newsletters

Follow any newsletter.

What are newsletter feeds?

Newsletter feeds let you follow any email newsletter directly inside Just…, the same way you follow an RSS feed. New editions appear in your Feeds tab automatically — no inbox required.

When a new edition arrives, it surfaces as a reading item you can queue, read, and reflect on like any other article.

Can I browse newsletters to find ones to follow?

Yes. Just… includes a built-in directory of 280+ curated newsletters across categories including Technology, Programming, AI, Design, Business, Security, and more.

Open the Feeds tab and tap Browse → Newsletters. Search by name or filter by category, then tap Subscribe on any newsletter. Just… takes it from there.

How do I follow a newsletter?

There are two ways. The quickest is the newsletter directory — tap Browse → Newsletters in the Feeds tab, find the one you want, and tap Subscribe.

To add one that isn't in the directory, tap + → Newsletter and paste the newsletter's website URL. Either way, the steps that follow are the same:

1. Just… opens the newsletter's sign-up form — in the app, not in Safari.
2. Give the feed a name in the form that loads.
3. Your reading address is generated and pinned at the top of the screen, already copied to your clipboard.
4. Paste the address into the subscription form on the page below and confirm.
5. Tap Done. The feed is added.

New editions appear in your Feeds tab automatically from that point on.

What is a reading address?

A reading address is a unique email address Just… generates for each newsletter you follow. You subscribe to the newsletter using that address instead of your personal inbox. When a new edition is sent to it, Just… picks it up and adds it to your feed automatically.

Your personal email address is never involved. The reading address is scoped to one newsletter and has no connection to any other account.

Which newsletters can I follow?

Any newsletter that lets you choose your own subscription email — which is almost all of them. Substack, Beehiiv, Ghost, Mailchimp, ConvertKit, and most other platforms accept any valid email address during signup.

If a newsletter is not in the built-in directory, add it manually via + → Newsletter and paste the newsletter's website URL.

How long until new editions appear?

Just… checks your newsletter feeds once daily. A new edition usually appears within a few hours of the newsletter sending, depending on when the daily fetch runs.

You can also tap the refresh button in the top-right corner of any newsletter's detail view to check for new editions immediately.

Where do I find my reading address again?

Long-press any newsletter in your Feeds tab and tap Copy reading address.

A newsletter edition isn't appearing.

First, confirm you subscribed using the reading address Just… generated — not your personal email. If you did, tap the refresh button in the newsletter's detail view to trigger an immediate fetch.

If editions still aren't appearing after 24 hours, the newsletter may not have sent to that address yet. Some newsletters take a few days to process new subscriptions. If the problem persists, write to us.

What happens if I remove a newsletter?

Swipe to delete or use the long-press menu in your Feeds tab. The newsletter and all its editions are removed from Just…. Future editions sent to the reading address are silently discarded — no action needed on the newsletter's side.

To stop receiving the newsletter entirely, use the unsubscribe link in any past edition.

The Brain

Your knowledge base.

What is the Brain?

The Brain is a personal record of everything you have read in Just…, along with any reflection you wrote. It grows with every entry and never shrinks — even if your streak breaks, your Brain keeps every entry.

What are Brain ranks?

As your Brain grows, it earns a new title: Curious (0–25 entries), Reader (26–100), Thinker (101–300), Scholar (301–750), Polymath (751–2,000), and Luminary (2,001+).

When your Brain earns a new rank, you'll see a quiet notification: "Your Brain is now a Thinker." No confetti — just the fact.

My Brain says it's full. What does that mean?

Free accounts can store up to 50 Brain entries. When you reach the cap, new entries are paused until you unlock Premium. All existing entries remain — nothing is deleted.

Premium is a one-time purchase that unlocks an unlimited Brain.

Can I export my Brain?

Yes, with Premium. You can export your entire Brain as a Markdown file — one entry per article, with title, date, and your reflection. The file opens in any text editor or note-taking app.

Where is my Brain stored? Is it backed up?

Your Brain is stored locally on your device and backed up automatically via iCloud if you have iCloud Drive enabled. Just… does not send your reflections to any external server.

The Streak

Daily reading, tracked simply.

How does the streak work?

Reading at least one article per day keeps the streak alive. There is no minimum reading time and no minimum reflection length. The streak resets at 3AM local time — not midnight — so late nights don't penalise you.

I missed a day. Is my streak gone?

Yes, but your Brain keeps every entry regardless of your streak status. The copy is simple: "Your streak ended. Start again." Nothing more. Pick up where you left off.

Why didn't my streak count update?

The streak counts full article reads, not just links added to the queue. Make sure you opened and read the article — not just added it. If you completed a read and the streak still didn't update, please write to us.

Can I turn off streak notifications?

Yes. Go to Settings → Notifications → Just… on your device and adjust permissions there. Just… only requests notification permission after your streak reaches 3 days — never on first launch.

Premium & purchases

Pricing and purchases.

What is Premium and how much does it cost?

Premium is a one-time in-app purchase — not a subscription. It unlocks an unlimited Brain, full-text Brain search, Markdown export, and a home screen widget. Pricing is displayed in the App Store for your region.

I bought Premium but it's not showing up.

Open Just…, go to Settings, and tap "Restore Purchases." This re-links your App Store account to your purchase. If the problem persists after restoring, write to us with your Apple ID email.

Can I use Just… on a new iPhone and keep my Brain?

Yes, provided iCloud Drive is enabled on both devices. Your Brain and streak history restore from iCloud on first launch. Premium also restores automatically — just tap "Restore Purchases."

Can I get a refund?

All purchases are handled by Apple and subject to Apple's refund policy. You can request a refund through Apple at reportaproblem.apple.com.

Technical

Common technical issues.

An article isn't loading correctly.

Some sites use JavaScript rendering or paywalls that prevent content from being fetched cleanly. When this happens, Just… opens a browser view so the content is still accessible.

If a site you use frequently isn't rendering correctly, let us know — we track common failures and work to improve the parser.

The Share Sheet extension isn't showing Just…

Tap "More" in the Share Sheet and scroll down to find Just…. If it's not listed, tap "Edit Actions" and enable it. This is a one-time step.

What iOS version does Just… require?

iOS 17 or later. Apple Intelligence reflect prompts require iOS 18.1 or later on a supported device.

Still stuck?

Write to us.

If your question isn't covered above, send a message. Include your iOS version and a description of the issue and we will get back to you within one business day.
